Tommy K. W. Ho

Biography

Tommy Ho is currently a graduate student of Computer Science pursuing the M.S. in Technology Innovation (MSTI) at the University of Washington. He is interested in understanding technical perspectives of product development and building technical solutions to real-world problems. He used to work in NVIDIA and several AI startups on practical AI deployment missions regarding object detection, semantic segmentation, practical machine learning on mobile phones / embedded devices, and chatbot development on Facebook Messenger. Besides his industry experience, he also had research experiences on natural language processing and computer vision when he was a research assistant at National Tsing Hua University. He published two papers regarding computer vision and distributed machine learning system on two distinguished international conferences during the time.

During his study at UW, he started introducing design thinking into his product development mindset. He is working with Microsoft AI for Good on a graduate launch project aimed at building an AI fundus camera to detect diabetic retinopathy used in developing countries. Besides the launch project, he also lays hands on all sorts of technology including the Internet of Things, frontend (e.g. HTML/CSS/Javascript), and backend development.

His résumé is available. Please feel free to contact him via e-mail or LinkedIn at any time!
